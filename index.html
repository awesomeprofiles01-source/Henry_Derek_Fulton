Getting to Know Me

HR Q&A

How do you ensure safety on the road?
Safety is not a checklist — it's a mindset. With 14 years of long-distance driving and zero incidents across my career, I pre-check every vehicle before a trip, manage fatigue proactively, and never compromise a schedule at the expense of road safety.
What sets you apart as a driver-mechanic combination?
Most drivers call a mechanic. I am the mechanic. When something sounds wrong on a long haul, I can diagnose it on the spot, often preventing a breakdown before it happens. That saves employers time, money, and frustration.
How do you handle pressure or unexpected breakdowns?
Calmly and practically. Years of working in remote or fast-paced environments taught me to assess the situation first, use what's available, and communicate clearly to those depending on the delivery. Panic doesn't fix engines — method does.
You've worked across very different industries. How do you adapt?
Transport, hospitality, agriculture — the environments change but the fundamentals don't: show up reliable, maintain the equipment, respect the team, and deliver results. I listen first, learn the context, then apply what I know.
What motivates you in your work?
I take genuine pride in a vehicle that runs clean, a delivery that arrives on time, and a job that needs no fixing after I've done it. Doing things properly the first time — that's my standard.
What are you looking for in your next role?
A stable position where my experience behind the wheel and under the bonnet is genuinely valued. I'm looking to contribute reliably to a team that takes maintenance and professionalism seriously, with room to grow.
